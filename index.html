1. Projection

  • The designer may try to build a 100% accurate representation of a globe.
  • However, when you are making a globe, or a portion of it, fit into a flat picture,
    some features will always be distorted: some distances, shapes, and area will be stretched, and others will be compressed.
  • There are five properties that can be distorted when project a globe on flat surface: shape, area, angles, distance, and direction.
  • At least three attributes will be sacrificed regardless of the projection you choose. Creating a flat map is a trade-off.
